Enrollment & Admission

We accept children from 12 months through age 12. Enrollment is ongoing throughout the year as spaces become available. Our popular programs fill up quickly, especially during summer and fall — we recommend applying early.

Our 7-step process:

  1. Schedule and attend a center tour
  2. Complete enrollment application (online or in-person)
  3. Submit required documents
  4. Receive acceptance letter
  5. Submit enrollment deposit & sign agreement
  6. Attend parent orientation
  7. Start day preparation

The entire process typically takes 2–4 weeks.

  • Proof of residency (utility bill, lease, or mortgage statement)
  • Child's birth certificate and social security card (copy)
  • Immunization records from your pediatrician
  • Physical exam records (within past 12 months)
  • Emergency contact form (signed)
  • Authorization for pickup form (signed)
  • Medical information form
  • Enrollment agreement (signed)
  • Payment method authorization

A deposit is required to secure your child's spot. Please contact us for current deposit details. The deposit is credited to your first payment once enrollment begins.

  • Full-Time: 5 days per week (most popular)
  • Part-Time: 2–4 days per week (discounted rate)
  • Drop-In Care: Available for current enrollees (subject to availability)
  • Custom Schedules: Contact us to discuss your needs

Health & Safety

Children should stay home if they have:

  • Fever of 100.4°F or higher (fever-free 24 hrs without medication to return)
  • Vomiting or diarrhea
  • Unexplained rash
  • Conjunctivitis/pink eye (until treated 24 hrs)
  • Strep throat or impetigo (until treated 24 hrs)

If a child becomes ill at our center, we call for pickup. Parents must arrive within 1 hour.

All children must have current immunizations per Texas DSHS guidelines. Required vaccines include DTP, Polio, MMR, Varicella, Hepatitis B, Hib, and Pneumococcal. Medical or religious exemptions can be discussed.

Medications: Administered only with written parent authorization, original packaging, and child's name and instructions clearly labeled.

Allergies: Detailed allergy info on file for each child. Staff trained on allergy protocols; separate meals/snacks prepared for food-allergic children. Severe allergies require an emergency action plan.

  • Secure Entry: All doors locked; keypad/key access only
  • Background Checks: All staff pass criminal background screening
  • Constant Supervision: Indoor and outdoor at all times
  • First Aid Trained: All staff certified
  • Emergency Drills: Monthly fire drills, biannual tornado drills
  • Authorized Pickup: Photo ID required for all pickups
  • Security Cameras: Common areas (not bathrooms)

Yes. We maintain regular sanitization of high-touch surfaces, hand hygiene education, updated illness policies per CDC and DSHS guidelines, and respiratory etiquette training. We notify families of any exposure or illness in the classroom.
