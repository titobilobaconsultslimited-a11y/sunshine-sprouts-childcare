Enrollment & Admission

We accept children from 6 weeks old through age 12. Enrollment is ongoing throughout the year as spaces become available. Our popular programs do fill up, especially during summer and fall sessions. We recommend scheduling a tour and applying early to secure your preferred start date.

Our enrollment process includes:

  1. Schedule and attend a center tour
  2. Complete enrollment application (online or in-person)
  3. Submit required documents (proof of residency, immunization records, emergency contact forms)
  4. Receive acceptance letter
  5. Submit enrollment deposit and sign enrollment agreement
  6. Attend parent orientation
  7. Start day preparation and planning

The entire process typically takes 2-4 weeks.

Required documents include:

  • Proof of residency (utility bill, lease, or mortgage statement)
  • Child's birth certificate and social security card (copy)
  • Immunization records from your pediatrician
  • Physical exam records (within past 12 months)
  • Emergency contact form (signed)
  • Authorization for pickup form (signed)
  • Medical information form
  • Enrollment agreement (signed)
  • Payment method authorization

An enrollment deposit is required to secure your child's spot and start date. Please contact us for current deposit details. The deposit is credited to your first payment once enrollment begins.

  • Part-Time: 2-4 days per week (discounted rate)

Programs & Curriculum

  • Infant (6 weeks - 12 months): 1 teacher per 4 infants
  • Toddler (12-24 months): 1 teacher per 5 toddlers
  • Preschool (2-4 years): 1 teacher per 8 children
  • After School (school-age 5-12 years): 1 teacher per 10 children
  • Summer Camp (all ages): Mixed-age groups with age-appropriate activities

We use a play-based, developmentally appropriate approach that emphasizes child-led learning through exploration and discovery. Our curriculum integrates:

  • Sensory and hands-on learning experiences
  • Language and literacy development
  • Math and logic concepts through play
  • Science and nature exploration
  • Social-emotional skill building
  • Creative expression through arts and music
  • Physical activity and outdoor play

Teachers observe children to understand individual interests and learning styles, then create experiences that build on their natural curiosity.

Yes! We provide nutritious meals and snacks daily:

  • Infants: Formula and age-appropriate solids (bring your preferred formula)
  • Toddlers & Preschool: Breakfast, lunch, and 2 snacks included
  • After School: Healthy snack daily

All meals meet USDA nutrition standards. We accommodate allergies and dietary restrictions (vegetarian, dairy-free, gluten-free, etc.). Weekly menus are posted in classrooms and on the parent portal.

Special Dietary Needs: Families can provide their own meals/snacks if preferred. Please provide in labeled, airtight containers.

Outdoor play is a critical part of our daily routine. Weather permitting, children go outside for at least 60 minutes daily, plus additional outdoor time during open-play periods. Outdoor play includes:

  • Playground equipment and climbing
  • Nature exploration and gardening
  • Sidewalk chalk, bubbles, and games
  • Sports and movement activities
  • Water play (warm months)

Please send weather-appropriate clothing daily. Closed-toe shoes are required for outdoor play.

Yes! We offer age-appropriate enrichment activities throughout the week:

  • Ages 3+: Yoga & Mindfulness (1x weekly)

Health & Safety

To keep our community healthy, children should stay home if they have:

  • Fever of 100.4°F or higher (must be fever-free for 24 hours without medication)
  • Vomiting or diarrhea
  • Unexplained rash
  • Cough or congestion
  • Conjunctivitis/pink eye (until treated for 24 hours)
  • Strep throat or impetigo (until treated for 24 hours)

If your child becomes ill while at our center, we call for immediate pickup. A staff member supervises your child in isolation. Parents must pick up within 1 hour.

All children must have current immunizations per Illinois Department of Public Health guidelines. Required vaccines include:

  • DTP (Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis)
  • Polio
  • MMR (Measles, Mumps, Rubella)
  • Varicella (Chickenpox)
  • Hepatitis B
  • Hib and Pneumococcal (for children under 5)

Medical or religious exemptions can be discussed. Immunization records must be current at enrollment and updated annually.

Medications: Staff can only administer medication with:

  • Written parent authorization
  • Original prescription or over-the-counter packaging
  • Clearly labeled with child's name and instructions

Allergies: We maintain detailed allergy information for each child. Staff are trained on allergy protocols. We prepare separate meals/snacks for children with food allergies and use separate utensils to prevent cross-contamination.

Severe Allergies: For severe/life-threatening allergies, an emergency action plan is required and reviewed with staff.

Child safety is our top priority. Our measures include:

  • Secure Building: All doors locked; authorized entry only via keypad/key
  • Background Checks: All staff pass criminal background screening
  • Supervision: Children supervised at all times (indoor and outdoor)
  • Trained Staff: First Aid trained and experienced in child development
  • Emergency Procedures: Fire drills monthly, tornado drills biannually, intruder response plan
  • Pickup Authorization: Only authorized adults can pick up; photo ID required
  • Cameras: Security cameras in common areas (not bathrooms)

Yes, we maintain health and safety standards including:

  • Regular sanitization of high-touch surfaces
  • Hand hygiene education and practices
  • Flexible scheduling for children with mild symptoms
  • Updated illness policies (per CDC and IDPH guidelines)
  • Respiratory etiquette training

We follow current public health guidance and notify families of any exposure or illness in the classroom.

Daily Operations & Routines

Sample Daily Schedule (Preschool):

  • 6:30-7:30 AM: Arrival & Free Play
  • 7:30-8:00 AM: Breakfast
  • 8:00-9:30 AM: Circle Time & Learning Activities
  • 9:30-10:00 AM: Snack Time
  • 10:00-11:30 AM: Outdoor Play
  • 11:30 AM-12:30 PM: Lunch & Rest Prep
  • 12:30-2:30 PM: Quiet Rest/Nap Time
  • 2:30-3:00 PM: Wake-up & Snack
  • 3:00-4:30 PM: Activity Time (Art, STEM, Music)
  • 4:30-6:30 PM: Free Play, Pickup

Schedules vary by age group. Toddlers have shorter activities and more nap/meal time. Infants follow individualized schedules.

Daily Items to Send:

  • Weather-appropriate clothing (jacket, hat, mittens, raincoat)
  • Closed-toe shoes (for outdoor play)
  • Extra set of clothes (2-3 in case of accidents)
  • Diaper/Pull-up supplies (if needed)
  • Blanket/comfort item for rest time
  • Any medications (with authorization forms)
  • Labeled backpack or bag

Do NOT send: Toys from home, candy, nuts/peanut products, or items with small parts.

We provide diaper supplies for infants and toddlers. However, we recommend families send:

  • Preferred brand of diapers (we use hypoallergenic, unscented brands)
  • Preferred wipes (fragrance-free recommended)
  • Diaper cream (if needed)

Items should be labeled with your child's name and brought in original packaging. We appreciate when families help replenish supplies, but there's no requirement to do so.

Communication Methods:

  • Parent App: Daily updates, photos, messages (for all ages)
  • Classroom Boards: In-classroom messages and weekly themes posted
  • Email: Center-wide announcements and monthly newsletters
  • Text Messages: Urgent notifications and emergency requests
  • In-Person: Quick conversations at pickup/drop-off
  • Conferences: Scheduled parent-teacher meetings 2x yearly

Infants receive daily reports including meals, sleep, and diaper changes. We keep parents informed about their child's development and activities!

Transitions & Special Support

We understand starting childcare is a big change! Our transition process includes:

  • Parent Orientation: Meet teachers, tour classroom, discuss your child's needs
  • Gradual Introduction: Recommend 1-2 hours first day, extending over 1 week
  • Comfort Items: Bring favorite toy, blanket, or family photo
  • Consistent Schedule: Same drop-off/pickup times help children feel secure
  • Daily Communication: Teachers share activities and mood via parent app
  • Separation Anxiety Support: We use positive goodbye routines and distraction techniques

Adjustment typically takes 2-3 weeks. Most children quickly learn that you always come back for them!

Yes! We welcome children with diverse needs. Our inclusive environment supports:

  • Speech/language delays
  • Physical disabilities and mobility needs
  • Hearing or vision impairments
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Behavioral or emotional challenges
  • Developmental delays

We'll work with your family, Early Intervention specialists, school districts, and therapists to create individualized support plans. Some children may require a classroom aide—this is discussed at enrollment.

We maintain wheelchair accessibility and adapted equipment as needed.

Absolutely! We support potty training when your child shows readiness signs (typically 2.5-3 years). Our approach includes:

  • Communication with parents about readiness indicators
  • Consistent potty routines throughout the day
  • Positive reinforcement and celebration of successes
  • No pressure or shame around accidents
  • Coordination between home and school for consistency

Daytime accidents are normal and expected. Pull-ups/diapers encouraged during transition period. Night-time training happens later and is usually a home-based process.

Pre-K classes have a specific focus on kindergarten readiness:

  • Academic Skills: Letter recognition, number concepts, pre-writing
  • Social Skills: Following directions, listening, cooperation, sharing
  • Self-Care: Bathroom independence, handwashing, eating etiquette
  • Kindergarten Visits: Field trips and speaking with kindergarten teachers
  • Parent Guidance: Tips for supporting school readiness at home
  • Records Sharing: Development portfolio shared with receiving school (with parent consent)

We work closely with local schools to ensure smooth transitions for our graduates!
