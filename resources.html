Center Policies

Click to expand and review our center policies and important procedures.

We care about the health of all children and staff. If your child shows signs of illness, we ask that you keep them home.

Children should stay home if they have:

  • Fever of 100.4°F or higher within the last 24 hours (without fever-reducing medication)
  • Diarrhea or vomiting
  • Rash with fever or behavior change
  • Severe cough or difficulty breathing
  • Strep throat or impetigo (until treated for 24 hours)
  • Conjunctivitis/pink eye (until treated or cleared by physician)
  • Suspected contagious illness

If your child becomes ill while at our center: We will contact you immediately to pick them up. A staff member will care for your child in a separate area until pickup. Please plan to pick up within one hour.

Return to Center: Children may return when fever-free for 24 hours without medication, or when cleared by a physician for contagious conditions.

Regular Hours: Monday - Friday, 6:30 AM - 6:30 PM. Classes are not available on weekends or major holidays.

Late Pickup Fees:

  • $1 per minute after 6:30 PM
  • Charged per child
  • Invoiced on next billing statement

Repeated Late Pickups: After 3 late pickups in one month, families will receive written notice and may be subject to enrollment review.

Emergency Field Closures: In case of severe weather, facility issues, or staff shortage, we will close. Notifications sent via email, phone, and our parent app ASAP.

Our Communication Channels:

  • Parent App: Daily updates, photos, messages, attendance
  • Email: Important announcements and policies
  • Text: Urgent notifications and pickups
  • Social Media: General center updates and celebration posts (no individual child photos without consent)

Privacy: We do not post identifiable information about individual children without parental consent. Check our photo release form.

Staff Social Media Policy: Staff will not connect with families on personal social media accounts. Communication is only through official center channels.

Our Philosophy: We use positive guidance to help children learn self-control and appropriate behavior.

Our Approach Includes:

  • Clear, age-appropriate expectations and routines
  • Redirection and coaching during conflicts
  • Natural consequences (e.g., cleaning up spills)
  • Problem-solving conversations
  • Celebration of positive behavior

Prohibited Methods: We never use harsh language, shame, physical punishment (including spanking), withholding meals/bathroom use, or isolation.

Ongoing Behavior Concerns: If challenges persist, we meet with parents to develop an individualized behavior plan.

We believe outdoor play is essential for child development. Children go outside daily unless prohibited by weather.

Weather Guidelines:

  • Extreme Cold: Below 15°F or wind chill below 0°F → Indoor play only
  • Extreme Heat: Above 92°F or heat index above 105°F → Shaded/indoor activities
  • Air Quality: Poor air quality days → Modified outdoor activities
  • Lightning Storms: All outdoor activities cease; move indoors immediately

What to Send: Please send weather-appropriate clothing (jackets, hats, mittens for cold; light layers for warm months). Closed-toe shoes required for outdoor play.

Annual Center Closures:

  • New Year's Day (January 1)
  • MLK Jr. Day (3rd Monday in January)
  • Presidents' Day (3rd Monday in February)
  • Memorial Day (Last Monday in May)
  • Independence Day (July 4)
  • Labor Day (1st Monday in September)
  • Thanksgiving & Day After (November)
  • Christmas & New Year's (December 24-25 & January 1)
  • Staff Professional Development Days (2 days yearly)

Tuition: Tuition remains the same regardless of closures. Families are not entitled to credits or discounts.

Calendar: Full closure calendar provided at enrollment and posted in parent areas.
