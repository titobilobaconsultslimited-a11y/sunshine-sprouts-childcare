Our Programs

Infant Care (6 weeks – 12 months)

Our infant program provides the warmth and attentiveness your newborn deserves. Each baby is assigned a primary caregiver who develops a deep understanding of their individual needs, rhythms, and preferences. We focus on building secure attachments while supporting early developmental milestones.

Daily Schedule

7:00 AM - 9:00 AM

Arrival & Morning Bonding - Individual attention, feeding, and greeting

9:00 AM - 11:00 AM

Tummy Time & Sensory Play - Developmental activities for motor skills

11:00 AM - 12:30 PM

Lunch & Nap Time - Nutrition and rest for healthy development

12:30 PM - 3:00 PM

Afternoon Activities - Music, high-contrast activities, gentle play

3:00 PM - 6:30 PM

Snack, Play & Pickup - Afternoon feeding and calm transition home

Curriculum Highlights

  • ✓ Responsive Caregiving: We respond to your baby's cues and communicate daily progress
  • ✓ Developmental Milestones: Activities designed for cognitive, motor, and social-emotional growth
  • ✓ Music & Sensory: Early exposure to sounds, textures, and rhythmic experiences

Program Details

Staff Ratio: 1 caregiver : 4 infants

Group Size: 8 infants

Hours: 6:30 AM - 6:30 PM (Full-time available)

Meals Included: Bottles, formula, snacks provided

Diapers: Provided (parents supply wipes)

Our Curriculum Philosophy

At NobleNest Daycare, we believe in play-based learning rooted in evidence-based developmental research. Our approach is guided by:

  • • Creative Curriculum: Integrated, project-based learning that celebrates children's interests
  • • HighScope Approach: Active learning with intentional teacher guidance
  • • Social-Emotional Learning: Building confidence, empathy, and emotional regulation
  • • Assessment: Ongoing observation and documentation to guide instruction

We believe every child is a capable, curious learner who develops best through play, meaningful relationships, and exploration in safe, respectful environments.

Research-Based

Our approach is grounded in current research on early childhood development and best practices from leading educational frameworks.
